About Aadyogi

Adiyogi is the originator of yoga. From Sanskrit, adi means “first” and yogi refers to a practitioner of yoga.

History

Adiyogi, meaning the "first yogi," is a title given to Lord Shiva, considered the originator of yoga. According to Hindu mythology, Shiva appeared thousands of years ago on the banks of the Kantisarovar Lake in the Himalayas, deeply immersed in meditation.Attracting many seekers, he imparted the knowledge of yoga to seven disciples, laying the foundation for various yoga systems. Adiyogi is symbolized in a meditative posture, representing enlightenment and inner peace. The 112-foot Adiyogi statue in Coimbatore, India, consecrated by the Isha Foundation.

Vision

Adiyogi's vision is about guiding individuals toward self-realization, inner peace, and universal consciousness through the practice of yoga. It emphasizes an inward journey to explore one's true essence, balancing physical, mental, and spiritual energies, and achieving harmony with the universe. Adiyogi's teachings promote holistic well-being, inclusivity, and timeless wisdom, encouraging personal growth and empowerment. Ultimately, his vision aims to create a world where individuals live in harmony with themselves and the universe.

Goals

Adiyogi's goals are to guide individuals towards self-realization and inner peace through yoga, emphasizing balance, harmony, and holistic well-being. He aims to make yoga's wisdom accessible to all, promoting personal growth and empowerment. Adiyogi envisions a world where individuals live in harmony with themselves and the universe, contributing positively to the world around them.
